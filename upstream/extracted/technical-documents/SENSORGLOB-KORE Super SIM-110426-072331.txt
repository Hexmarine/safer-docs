KORE Super SIM
We use KORE (previously Twilio) Super SIM for our cellular connectivity.
  These SIM cards automatically connect to any available network we’ve allowed.
  For the AU Pilot Fleet, the available networks are Telstra, Optus and Vodafone.
  The SIM will initially attempt to connect to Telstra, as it’s the only item in the OPLMN, as per
  Twilio support:

   Regarding your question on OPLMN list,
   U.S. AT&T
   Australia Telstra
   New Zealand Spark
  If it fails to connect to Telstra, it will then try Optus and Vodafone.
  Once connected it will re-connect to the last connected network initially, then continue
  through the list.

Data Usage


Regarding your question "Is there a minimum usage or connection time for these to show in this
report? Do SIM cards in the Ready state show up in this report, or do they only show once they
progress to the Active state?"

Data usage will show for active sims and not sims in ready status.

  https://www.twilio.com/docs/iot/supersim/how-to-determine-a-super-sims-status

  network resources in accordance with the configuration of the Fleet resource to which it has
  been assigned (indicated by the Sim resource’s fleet_sid property), but no monthly
  subscription fee will be charged.
   A Sim resource’s status can only be switched to ready if it currently new . Once
  a ready Super SIM has consumed 250KB of data, five SMS Commands have been sent or
  received by the Super SIM, or three months have passed, the Sim resource’s state will
  automatically transition to active .
